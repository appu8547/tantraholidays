Discover The Beautiful World

Alapuzha

Referred to as the Venice of the East, Alappuzha has always enjoyed an important place in the maritime history of Kerala. Today, it is famous for its boat races, backwater holidays, beaches, marine products and coir industry. Alappuzha Beach is a popular picnic spot. The pier, which extends out to the sea here, is over 137 years old. Entertainment facilities at the Vijaya Beach Park add to the attraction of the beach. There is also an old lighthouse nearby which greatly intrigues all visitors.

Munnar

Nestled amidst the rolling hills of the Western Ghats in Kerala, Munnar stands as a picturesque paradise, renowned for its lush tea estates, mist-clad mountains, and pristine valleys. As one of South India's most sought-after hill stations, Munnar offers visitors a serene escape from the hustle and bustle of city life. Trekking through its verdant trails, exploring the tea gardens, and indulging in the breathtaking vistas of cascading waterfalls and meandering streams are just a few of the experiences that await travelers in this tranquil haven. Whether it's witnessing the sunrise from atop the towering peaks, strolling through fragrant spice plantations, or simply reveling in the tranquility of its scenic landscapes, Munnar captivates the hearts of all who venture into its embrace.

Wayanad

Wayanad, the green paradise is nestled among the mountains of the Western Ghats, forming the border world of the greener part of Kerala. Clean and pristine, enchanting and hypnotizing, this land is filled with history and culture. Located at a distance of 76 km. from the sea shores of Kozhikode, this verdant hill station is full of plantations, forests and wildlife

Kovalam

Nestled along the Arabian Sea in Kerala, is a paradise for travellers seeking a coastal retreat filled with sun, sand, and sea. Kovalam offers a myriad of experiences that will leave you enthralled and rejuvenated. Whether you seek adventure, relaxation, or cultural exploration, this coastal town has it all. Embrace the tranquillity of its beaches, the serenity of its backwaters, and the warmth of its people. Come and indulge in the delights of Kovalam, where every moment is a picture-perfect memory waiting to be captured.

Nilambur

About 53 kilometres from Malappuram lies Nilambur, a place known for teak plantations and tribal settlements. Proximity to the Nilgiri ranges of Western Ghats blesses Nilambur with ample rainfall, waterfalls and lush green rain forests. In addition to teak, there are many mahogany, rosewood and bamboo plantations here as well.

Athirappily

Nestled along the enchanting landscapes of Kerala, Athirappilly enchants visitors with its majestic waterfalls and lush greenery. Renowned as the "Niagara of India," Athirappilly waterfall cascades gracefully amidst dense forests, offering a mesmerizing spectacle of nature's grandeur. Visitors can immerse themselves in the serene ambiance, embark on scenic walks through the surrounding wilderness, or enjoy thrilling boat rides along the pristine river. Beyond its natural splendor, Athirappilly also boasts rich biodiversity, making it a haven for wildlife enthusiasts and photographers. With its captivating beauty and tranquil ambiance, Athirappilly promises an unforgettable experience for those seeking solace in nature's embrace.

Palakkad

Port Blair is an alluring destination for tourists as it includes a host of attractive locales. There are shimmering but clean beaches, beaches that defy the ferocity of sea and let tourists swim leisurely, relics of colonial power and oppression, many must-go museums, libraries, coral reefs, and more.

Thekkady

Palakkad, often dubbed the "Gateway of Kerala," is a picturesque district nestled between the Western Ghats and the plains of Tamil Nadu. Renowned for its lush greenery and extensive paddy fields, it boasts a rich cultural heritage influenced by Malayali, Tamil, and Kannadiga traditions. Visitors are drawn to iconic landmarks like the Palakkad Fort and the Malampuzha Dam, while the cuisine, blending Kerala and Tamil Nadu flavors, offers a culinary delight. With its agrarian economy, diverse attractions, and excellent transportation links, Palakkad presents a captivating destination for travelers seeking natural beauty, cultural immersion, and historical intrigue.
